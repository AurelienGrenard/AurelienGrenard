## Research

### Differentiable Generative Models for Financial Simulation

A major part of my PhD, carried out in collaboration with **Lokman Abbas-Turki**, focused on the use of **generative models for stochastic financial dynamics**.

The objective was to construct accurate and differentiable neural simulators capable of learning distributions generated by classical stochastic models while providing fast simulation and efficient sensitivity computation. This work connects generative modeling with Monte Carlo methods, stochastic models, option pricing, and calibration.

**Code and paper:** [GAN-SDE](../GAN-SDE)

---

### Sobolev Training and Deep Calibration

Another major part of my PhD, also developed in collaboration with **Lokman Abbas-Turki**, focused on **Sobolev training for neural pricing functions** and **deep calibration of financial models**.

I investigated the use of derivative information during training and compared classical value-based learning with Sobolev-type objectives incorporating gradients. The work studies the resulting accuracy of prices and sensitivities, the preservation of static arbitrage properties, and the use of differentiable neural pricers for fast gradient-based calibration.

The methodology was studied across several classical and rough financial models, including Heston, rough Bergomi, Variance Gamma, and Normal Inverse Gaussian models.

**Code and paper:** [Deep-Calibration-and-Sobolev-Training](../Deep-Calibration-and-Sobolev-Training)

---

### Anticipated Backward Stochastic Differential Equations

In parallel, I worked with **Stéphane Crépey** and **Cyril Bénézet** on **anticipated backward stochastic differential equations (anticipated BSDEs)**.

This research concerns stochastic equations whose dynamics depend on future values of the solution and combines stochastic analysis with learning-based numerical approximation.

**Code and paper:** [Anticipated-BSDE](../Anticipated-BSDE)

---

### Stochastic Impulse Control

I also worked on **numerical methods for multidimensional stochastic impulse control problems**, in collaboration with **Lokman Abbas-Turki** and **Idris Kharroubi**.

The project develops an approximation methodology based on **neural regression and randomized optimization** for computing solutions to high-dimensional impulse control problems.

**Code and paper:** [Impulse-control](../Impulse-control)
